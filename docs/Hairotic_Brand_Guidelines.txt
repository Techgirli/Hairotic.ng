
HAIROTIC.NG
BRAND GUIDELINES
Version 1.0  •  Prepared by Miracle  •  Status: Draft
Premium hair, shopped like a fashion drop.


1. Brand Mission
To empower every woman to express her boldest, most confident self through premium hair that turns heads, inspires confidence, and never holds her back.
2. Vision Statement
To become Nigeria's most loved premium hair brand, known for bold style, uncompromising quality, and unforgettable customer experiences — starting in Lagos and inspiring confidence nationwide.
3. Brand Values
Boldness — every page, product, and post makes a statement; never generic
Premium quality — high-quality photography, real materials, no shortcuts
Trust — transparent pricing, real reviews, clear policies
Human warmth — friendly, helpful, never cold or corporate
Speed and simplicity — customers reach checkout in a few clicks, never left guessing
4. Brand Voice & Tone
Voice
Confident. Playful. Friendly. Authentic. Supportive.
Never sound
Corporate. Cold. Pushy. Overly salesy.
In practice
Product copy: "This unit lays flat, holds curl for weeks, and yes — it's as soft as it looks."
Checkout microcopy: "Your order's safe with us — free swaps within 7 days."
Social captions: punchy and fun. Website: warm and clear. Customer service: reassuring and expert.
5. Target Audience
Trend-driven Nigerian women, 18–35, shopping primarily via phone, who see hair as a style statement and are willing to pay for quality but want to see it justified through strong photography, real reviews, and clear policies. They compare Hairotic against both premium salons and Instagram/WhatsApp hair vendors, so the site must feel more trustworthy and polished than either.
6. Unique Value Proposition
Premium hair, shopped like a fashion drop — bold styles, honest pricing, and a checkout experience that never keeps you guessing.
7. Brand Personality
Bold  •  Stylish  •  Confident  •  Youthful  •  Creative  •  Honest  •  Premium

8. Color System

--- Table Start ---
Color | Hex | Usage
Deep Orange | #E56717 | Primary buttons, links, highlights, icons, active navigation, progress bars
White | #FFFFFF | Backgrounds, cards, forms, sections
Charcoal | #222222 | Text, icons, navigation
Gray | #6B7280 | Secondary text, labels, descriptions
Light Gray | #F8F8F8 | Cards, section backgrounds
--- Table End ---


Deep orange reads as energetic and confident while staying premium rather than cheap or loud. Paired with white space and charcoal (instead of pure black), the palette feels editorial and fashion-forward — bold and premium at once, which is the hardest balance to strike in hair ecommerce.
9. Typography
Display — Rubik Spray Paint
Use only for: logo, hero statements, campaign headlines, large headings. Never body text.
Primary — TASA Explorer
Weights: Regular, Medium, SemiBold, Bold. Carries all headings (H1–H5), body copy, labels, and buttons.

10. Logo Usage Guidelines

Top-left navigation
Footer
Checkout
Packaging
Emails
Invoices
Order tracking
Minimum spacing
At least the height of the "H" on all sides.
Never
Stretch
Rotate
Change colors
Add shadows
Add outlines
Small-size use
For favicons and app icons, use a simplified icon/monogram version rather than the full wordmark — Rubik Spray Paint loses legibility at very small sizes.

11. Design Principles

--- Table Start ---
Principle | What it means
Bold | Every page makes a statement. Never generic.
Premium | Lots of whitespace, high-quality photography, minimal clutter.
Human | Warm language, helpful guidance, friendly interactions.
Fast | Users should reach checkout within a few clicks.

Every page must answer three questions for the user:
Where am I?
What can I do?
What happens next? — Never leave users guessing.
